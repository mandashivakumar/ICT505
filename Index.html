How Starbucks Uses Social Media for Customer Knowledge Management

Based on "Customer knowledge management via social media: the case of Starbucks"

The Modern Caf√©: Coffee, Community & Knowledge

Knowledge FOR Customers

Informing customers about products, campaigns, and news.

Knowledge FROM Customers

Listening to feedback, ideas, preferences, and complaints.

Knowledge ABOUT Customers

Analyzing conversations and behavior to gain insights.

Starbucks uses a diverse social media strategy to manage these three key knowledge flows.

Twitter: Real-Time Engagement

FOR Customers: Announcing product launches (e.g., "Pumpkin Spice Latte"), promoting campaigns, and providing direct customer service.

FROM Customers: Actively seeking feedback via tweets and identifying customer likes and dislikes.

ABOUT Customers: Monitoring conversations to understand concerns and quickly address rumors or misconceptions.

Facebook: Building Community

FOR Customers: Sharing info on products, culture, and major updates (e.g., mobile payments). Promoting offers like "Frappuccino Happy Hour".

FROM Customers: Using polls to gather opinions and monitoring comments to understand customer reactions.

ABOUT Customers: Understanding customer behaviors, preferences, and satisfaction levels by analyzing engagement.

Location-Aware Services: Hyper-Local

FOR Customers: Providing location-specific offers and encouraging check-ins through badges and statuses.

FROM Customers: Collecting tips and comments about specific store locations (e.g., layout, service quality).

ABOUT Customers: Journal article found no specific evidence of Starbucks using Foursquare to manage knowledge *about* customers directly.

MyStarbucksIdea: Co-Creation

FOR Customers: Providing status updates on submitted ideas (e.g., "Under Review," "Launched"), showing that they are being heard.

FROM Customers: Gathering suggestions and novel ideas for improvement, promoting "design with customers".

ABOUT Customers: Treating customers as evaluators and using their votes and comments to gauge the popularity of new ideas.

A Multi-Platform CKM Strategy

  • Starbucks tailors its approach to the unique strengths of each platform.
  • The strategy moves beyond broadcasting to a two-way conversation and analysis.
  • Customers are treated as partners and co-creators, building deep brand loyalty.
  • This integrated approach allows Starbucks to remain agile, innovative, and responsive.
